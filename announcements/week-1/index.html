Spiritual Thought/Question for the Week

I want to share with you some truths about learning. This is something that I learned years ago and it has helped. There are a couple of scriptures I want to point you too. The first thing you need to understand is that all truth belongs to the doctrine of the kingdom. President Brigham Young taught "If you can find a truth in heaven, earth or hell, it belongs to our doctrine. We believe it; it is ours; we claim it" (DBY, 2). This does not mean just spiritual knowledge but secular knowledge as well.

The first scripture is found in D&C 88:77-79. In these versus we find the commandment about gaining an education and how to teach one-another. 

77 And I give unto you a commandment that you shall teach one another the doctrine of the kingdom.

78 Teach ye diligently and my grace shall attend you, that you may be instructed more perfectly in theory, in principle, in doctrine, in the law of the gospel, in all things that pertain unto the kingdom of God, that are expedient for you to understand;

79 Of things both in heaven and in the earth, and under the earth; things which have been, things which are, things which must shortly come to pass; things which are at home, things which are abroad; the wars and the perplexities of the nations, and the judgments which are on the land; and a knowledge also of countries and of kingdoms

Verse 79 gives us a great list of things to learn in our lifetime. Think of the terminology used to day for each items in that list: "In the heavens" for example are things like astronomy, space flight, satellites, etc.

Notice it does not say to pick one or two things but to learn all.  This is one of BYU-Idaho's principles, teaching you to become a lifelong learner.

The second scripture is found in Alma 37:8. This is when Alma is explaining to his son Helaman about the writing on the plates and what his charge will be in keeping the record of his people. Verse 8 gives us a very helpful piece of information about how we can retain what we learn. I am bolding the part of the scripture I will be focusing on.

8 And now, it has hitherto been wisdom in God that these things should be preserved; for behold, they have enlarged the memory of this people, yea, and convinced many of the error of their ways, and brought them to the knowledge of their God unto the salvation of their souls.

The word THEY is referring to the scriptures that Alma has been talking about. In other words, by studying the scriptures, you will be blessed with a greater memory and be able to recall things to your mind. I promise you, if you keep studying the scriptures everyday along with your schoolwork, you will be able to remember more and do better in school. It will take time to show your faithfulness to be able to get this blessing.
