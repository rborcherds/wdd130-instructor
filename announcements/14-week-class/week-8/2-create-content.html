The most common tips for good content

  • Email vs. e-mail, Internet vs. internet and other style debates - You must keep up with what is the common and correct terminology used on the internet
  • Make the reader feel something - almost all viral writing shares one thing in common: emotional impact.
  • Keep the action in your content writing - Writing for the web should be powerful, direct and punchy. To do that, your sentence structure, word choice and style need to emphasize action.
  • When writing for the web, chop it up - use less than 20 word sentences and no more than 3 sentences per paragraph for the web content.
  • Give your readers a reason to care from the first sentence.

"Content writing tip: your website isn't literature. Your readers are there to solve a problem, not gush about rhetorical devices. #marketing" - The Content Factory

  • Paint a picture
  • Keep the reading level low - Try to keep the reading level at 7th or 8th grade reading level. The majority in this grade range are 13 and 14 years old.
  • Stick to active voice - There are a few sites that will help you with this. Here are 2 of them.  Search for "active voice checker" in your favorite search engine to find more.
    https://datayze.com/passive-voice-detector
    https://www.activevoicechecker.com/
  • Nix the jargon - Spell out acronyms on first reference. Avoid insider language. Explain complex or niche terms, etc.
  • Mix up your word choice - Here’s the exception: keep key terms consistent across your site to avoid confusing your visitors. For example, if you’re a photographer, don’t offer “photoshoots” on one page then call them “photography sessions” on the next
  • Help Readers Navigate
  • Talk to Readers as You Would a Friend
  • A void Spelling, Grammar, or Punctuation Errors - Use Google Docs or your favorite word processor and use the English spell and grammer checker that is in every single word processor. You can also log into the Office365 accounting using your school login and use Word online for this as well.
  • Don’t use images that you don’t have a legal right to use - This is also part of the BYU-Idaho honor code.
  • Sound like a human!
  • Make sure the content on your web pages can be scanned quickly
