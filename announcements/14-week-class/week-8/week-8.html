Personal Site Content

This week you will be writing all the content for your personal site. You will also be finding images for your site. Make sure you follow the instructions on these two items carefully.

  1. Significant content. This generally means at least 300 words of content for each page. The more the better as it will give you more options on what you can place on your web pages.
  2. Images. Make sure at least one image per page relate to the topic of that page. I will be randomly checking to make sure none of the images are copyrighted. This issue of using copyrighted images is of big concern for students. You will learn some of the legalities of this issue in a few classes. You will have to take a law class to learn all the aspects of this issue. Just be aware that copyright infringement could cost you thousands to millions of dollars to settle. Just better not do it.
  3. Free image sites. I give you this warning about using these sites. I use them a lot and this is from experience. A lot of them will have images that are not conducive to the teachings of the church. There is a lot of nudity on some of them. Just be aware and be cautious. There are hundreds of them on the internet with some free content and a lot of paid content. Most free sites will require you to sign-up with them to be able to download the images. Do not use your BYU-I email address for these sites.
  4. All the text and optimized images must be added to the site plan and submitted.

Spiritual Challenge

For the first time ever, The temple recommend questions are now found in the Handbook of Instruction, Chapter 26, section 26.3.3.1

https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/general-handbook/26-temple-recommends?lang=eng&id=title_number30#title_number30

These are the first 4 questions.

 

  1. Do you have faith in and a testimony of God, the Eternal Father; His Son, Jesus Christ; and the Holy Ghost?
  2. Do you have a testimony of the Atonement of Jesus Christ and of His role as your Savior and Redeemer?
  3. Do you have a testimony of the Restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ?
  4. Do you sustain the President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints as the prophet, seer, and revelator and as the only person on the earth authorized to exercise all priesthood keys?
    Do you sustain the members of the First Presidency and the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators?
    Do you sustain the other General Authorities and local leaders of the Church?

The discussion this week is:

What is your understanding of these questions in your life? How do you know if you have faith, testimony, or sustain as these questions ask?
