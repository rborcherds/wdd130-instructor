Feedback

As we go along with these assignments, I will be very direct with my feedback. It is nothing personal towards any one of you. There are two types of feedback you need to learn about. You will be giving feedback to other students in almost every class you take in one form or another at BYU-Idaho. There is constructive and destructive criticism. I will always do my best to give you constructive criticism. If you do not understand the feedback I give you, times I will use terms that are common but you may not understand the meaning of the term. Ask so you can learn. I may suggest things to do with your code now that will not make sense. I make these suggestions to help get your mind in a mode that will make learning other principles easier in the future.

 

I am a web developer for clients as part of my everyday work. Feedback from clients can be very harsh.

When it comes to web development, the smallest details matter. Let me give you an example from a website I coded for someone about 5 years ago. This was done in WordPress, which I do not use or recommend. This client was not nice about the feedback and they way they communicated back to me. The design I was given had the following type of icon in it with the arrow pointing to the right. 

The customer was not happy with the way the icon and text that was to the right of the icon ended up looking like on the web. The first line of text was not directly in line with the arrow. Based upon how much text there was, the starting of the text moved higher and higher towards the top of the icon. Because of the way alignment works on the web, what they were asking was not possible. This type of alignment changes on various devices, which was another issue for the client. The desktop had the arrow and text to the right and the cell phone did not have the text to the right because the icon was above the text on the cell phone.

To make the client happy, the icon was changed to be one without the little arrow on all devices.

 

Even though they accepted the original design thinking the web was exactly as a graphics program, which was done the way they thought it should be. The designer did not know about the limitation on the web for alignment. This created an issue that I ended up having to deal with. For the client it was a visual thing only. It did not allow for their OCD (Obsessive Compulsive Disorder) personality to accept the way it worked by default.

You will have people like this to deal with in any type of designing that you end up doing. I have found this to be a part of web development life.

These small details matter and the better you can get at identifying the details the fewer changes you will have to make to the layout of the page to make your clients/bosses happy with the design.

I give detailed feedback as often as I can like this. Pay attention to it.  A lot of the feedback is in the comments on your submissions.
